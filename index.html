About

Kavid Do was born in 1997 in Seoul, South Korea as David Ko. He grew up in a Vancouver suburb, where he went to church, learned to play the piano and compose. In 2014-15 he studied composition privately with Rodney Sharman. In 2015, he moved to Amsterdam to study at the Conservatorium van Amsterdam, where he studied with Joël Bons and Richard Ayres (BMus). He is currently completing his MMus in composition, and studies with Jorrit Tamminga and Richard Ayres. He has previously studied Classical piano with David Kuyken.

His music combines extended just intonation, recording technique, and sound design, in search of dreamlike sounds that evoke nostalgia and memories of childhood, in his case one of digital frenzies and functional, protestant aesthetics. His music has been performed in halls such as Korzo, Muziekgebouw aan 't IJ and the Orpheum Annex in Vancouver. He has collaborated with visual artists Monica Mays and Min Oh and theatre director Rebekka Nilsson, both as a composer and performer.

He has received scholarships from the Langley Community Music School, British Columbia Arts Council, and the Amsterdam School of the Arts. In 2018, his piece Let's Talk About Heritage received the SOCAN young composer's award.

Works

Psalm Songs (Work-in-progress)

Music can communicate the memory of lived experiences through associative sounds that stimulate both the spirit and the viscera. Instruments, acoustic and digital, offer tones to contribute to collective melodies, ranging from sober and clear, to hoarse and fragile. Do these melodies embody and reflect some ethical code, or have they simply come from the beyond?
